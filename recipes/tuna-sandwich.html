Tuna Sandwiches: Tuna Salad or Tuna Melt

Canned tuna is one of my favorite protein sources. It's easier than cooking raw meat or fish, and it can be surprisingly tasty if you treat it right. Since tinned tuna is unbearably dry on its own, mixing in a tablespoon or so of mayo adds some fat to the party and results in a more pleasing texture.

Tuna Salad template

Ingredients

  • Canned tuna
  • 1-2 Tbsp mayo per can of tuna
  • A healthy glob of mustard
  • Seasonings: salt, pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, dried herbs, a pinch of cayenne if you're feelin' spicy
  • Chopped celery (I usually don't include this because I'm lazy, but it provides a nice crunch)
  • A chopped up pickle or lemon juice for some acidity

Just mix all these ingredients and you're good to go! From here the possibilities are endless. Here are some of my favorite ways to use this tuna salad:

  • Use as a dip for crackers or toasts
  • For a classic tuna salad sandwich, spread the tuna salad on a slice of bread and top with lettuce, tomato, and another slice of bread.
  • For a diner-style tuna melt, add a slice of cheese to a tuna salad sandwich, and toast it in a pan until both sides are browned and the cheese is melted.
